Ice Rinks

The Natural History Museum

Line: Piccadilly (also Circle and District)
Station: South Kensington

Distance from tube: 5 minute walk

Ice Skating at the Natural History Museum

It doesn’t matter if you’re a pro or if you’ve never skated before, the backdrop of the Natural History Museum’s Waterhouse building makes the ice rink magical for everyone. Enjoy a bird’s eye view from the Café Bar as you warm up with a hot drink post-skate.

Somerset House

Line: Circle (also District)
Station: Temple

Distance from tube: 5 minute walk

Right in the heart of London, Somerset House’s ice rink is open day and night for skating with family and friends (including two dedicated skating sessions for wheelchair users). The rink’s home to a number of “Skate Lates” music nights featuring a variety of artists and DJs, or for the kids there’s a chance to “Skate with Snoopy” in celebration of the Somerset House-run exhibition “Good Grief, Charlie Brown!”

Canary Wharf

Line: Jubilee
Station: Canary Wharf

Distance from tube: 2 minute walk

One of the longest-running seasonal ice rinks in London, Canary Wharf’s 1200 sq. m outdoor ice rink in Canada Square Park has an 8km grid of LED lights underneath the ice which is sure to light up your life! With a rink-side bar you can stay close to the action or visit one of the many shops, bars and restaurants surrounding the rink.
